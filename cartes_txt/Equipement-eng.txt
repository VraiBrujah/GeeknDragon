They are compact, durable, and essential for sustaining adventurers on long journeys.\n\n---\n\n**Mechanics:**\n\n>- **Daily Sustenance:** One ration provides enough food to meet the daily requirement for a **Small** or **Medium** creature.**Malnutrition Hazard:**\n\n>- A creature must consume at least **half** its required daily food to avoid **Exhaustion**.\n\n>- Eating less than half requires a **DC 10 Constitution saving throw** or the creature gains **1 level of Exhaustion** at the end of the day.\n\n>- A creature eating nothing for **5 consecutive days** gains **1 level of Exhaustion automatically** on the fifth day, and an additional level for each subsequent day without food.\n\n>- **Exhaustion Recovery:** Eating a full day’s worth of food removes malnutrition-related Exhaustion.\n\n>- **Flavor:** While functional and compact, rations are bland and repetitive. They are meant for survival, not enjoyment.\n\n---\n\n**Food Needs per Day**: A **Tiny** creature requires **1/4 lb (0.1 kg)** of food per day, a **Small or Medium** creature **1 lb (0.5 kg)**, a **Large** creature **4 lb (2 kg)**, a **Huge** creature **16 lb (8 kg)**, and a **Gargantuan** creature **64 lb (32 kg)**.  \n\n---\n\n**Exhaustion Condition**:\n\n>  - **Cumulative Levels**: Each time you gain Exhaustion, you receive **1 level of Exhaustion**. At **6 levels**, the character dies.\n\n>  - **D20 Tests Affected**: Reduce the result of all **D20 Tests** (ability checks, saving throws, and attack rolls) by **2 × your Exhaustion level**.\n\n>  - **Speed Reduced**: Reduce your **Speed** by **5 feet (1.5 m, 1 case)** per Exhaustion level.
